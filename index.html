Espresso Machine Troubleshooting Guide

Expert solutions for the 20 most common espresso machine problems — from leaks and water flow to taste and pressure issues.

1. Why is my espresso machine not pumping water?

Common causes: Empty tank, clogged filter, air lock, or faulty pump.
Solution: Fill and seat tank, clean intake filter, purge air by running water without portafilter. If no sound, pump may need replacement.

2. Why is my espresso coming out too slowly?

Causes: Too fine grind, overfilled basket, over-tamping, or clogging.
Solution: Coarsen grind, reduce dose (18–20g), tamp evenly (~30lb), clean shower screen.

3. Why is my espresso weak or watery?

Causes: Too coarse grind, stale beans, under-dosing.
Solution: Grind finer, use fresh beans (2–4 weeks), check dose and tamp consistency.

4. Why is my espresso machine leaking water?

Causes: Worn gasket, cracked tank seal, or internal tubing.
Solution: Replace gasket/seal annually; internal leaks require professional service.

5. Why is my espresso machine not heating up?

Causes: Tripped fuse, thermostat, heating element, or heavy scale.
Solution: Descale thoroughly, check fuses, seek professional repair if no recovery.

6. Why is my steam wand not working?

Causes: Clogged steam tip or low boiler pressure.
Solution: Soak in descaler, purge before and after use, service if no steam pressure builds.

7. How do I descale my espresso machine?

Steps:
1. Fill tank with descaling mix.
2. Run brew cycle halfway.
3. Let sit 15–20 minutes.
4. Run remaining water.
5. Rinse 2–3 times with clean water.

8. Why does my espresso taste bitter?

Causes: Over-extraction, too hot water, dirty equipment.
Solution: Coarsen grind, clean parts, verify brew temperature (195–205°F).

9. Why does my espresso taste sour?

Causes: Under-extraction or too cool water.
Solution: Grind finer, warm machine, use fresh beans.

10. My grinder runs but no coffee comes out.

Causes: Burr jam, cracked carrier, or worn burrs.
Solution: Check for jammed beans or stones, replace worn burrs.
