1 Why is my espresso machine not pumping water?

Common Causes: Empty water tank or improperly seated tank, clogged water intake filter, air lock in the pump system, or faulty pump.
Solution: First, check that the water tank is full and properly seated - it should click into place. Remove the tank and clean the water intake filter (usually a small screen at the bottom). Run the machine without a portafilter attached to purge any air from the system. If the pump makes no noise at all when you try to brew, the pump may have failed and needs replacement.

This solves the problem 85% of the time. If your pump is running but no water flows after trying these steps, professional service may be needed.

2 Why is my espresso coming out too slowly?

Common Causes: Coffee ground too fine, too much coffee in the portafilter, over-tamping, or clogged shower screen/group head.
Solution: Adjust your grinder to a coarser setting (move one setting at a time). Use the correct dose - typically 18-20g for a double shot basket. Tamp with consistent pressure (about 30 pounds). Remove and clean the shower screen and check the group head gasket for coffee buildup. A proper extraction should take 25-30 seconds for a 2oz double shot.

3 Why is my espresso coming out too fast or watery?

Common Causes: Coffee ground too coarse, insufficient coffee dose, inadequate tamping, or stale coffee beans.
Solution: Grind your coffee finer (adjust one setting at a time). Ensure you're using the proper dose for your basket size. Tamp evenly with 30 lbs of pressure - the puck should be level and firm. Most importantly, use fresh coffee beans roasted within the last 2-4 weeks. Old beans lose CO2 and won't produce proper extraction resistance.

4 Why is my espresso machine leaking water?

Common Causes: Worn group head gasket, loose portafilter, damaged water tank seal, or cracked internal tubing.
Solution: The group head gasket should be replaced every 6-12 months depending on use. Make sure your portafilter locks tightly into the group head. Check the water tank for damaged seals and replace if necessary. During descaling, inspect visible internal hoses for cracks. Leaks from the bottom of the machine often indicate internal tube failure requiring professional repair.
