1. Why is my espresso machine not pumping water?

Common causes: Empty water tank, clogged intake filter, air lock in system, or faulty pump motor.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Check and fill water tank, ensure it's properly seated
‚Ä¢ Clean water intake filter (usually at tank connection)
‚Ä¢ Purge air by running hot water without portafilter
‚Ä¢ Listen for pump noise - no sound may indicate pump failure requiring professional repair

2. Why is my espresso coming out too slowly?

Causes: Too fine grind, overfilled basket, excessive tamping pressure, or clogged shower screen.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Adjust grinder to coarser setting
‚Ä¢ Use proper dose (18-20g for double shot)
‚Ä¢ Apply consistent 30lb tamping pressure
‚Ä¢ Clean shower screen and group head weekly

3. Why is my espresso weak or watery?

Causes: Too coarse grind, stale beans, under-dosing, or insufficient tamping.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Grind finer until extraction takes 25-30 seconds
‚Ä¢ Use fresh beans (roasted within 2-4 weeks)
‚Ä¢ Verify dose weight with scale
‚Ä¢ Ensure level, firm tamping

4. Why is my espresso machine leaking water?

Causes: Worn group head gasket, cracked water tank, loose connections, or damaged internal tubing.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Replace group gasket annually (DIY possible)
‚Ä¢ Check tank for hairline cracks
‚Ä¢ Tighten all visible connections
‚Ä¢ Internal leaks require professional service

5. Why is my espresso machine not heating up?

Causes: Tripped thermal fuse, faulty thermostat, broken heating element, or heavy scale buildup.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Perform thorough descaling first
‚Ä¢ Check power outlet and machine fuses
‚Ä¢ Allow 15-20 minutes warmup time
‚Ä¢ If no improvement, heating element likely needs replacement

6. Why is my steam wand not working?

Causes: Clogged steam tip, milk residue blockage, or insufficient boiler pressure.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Soak tip in hot water or descaling solution
‚Ä¢ Use pin to clear steam holes
‚Ä¢ Always purge before and after steaming
‚Ä¢ Check boiler pressure gauge (1-1.5 bar normal)

7. How do I descale my espresso machine?

Complete descaling process:
1. Mix descaling solution with water per manufacturer instructions
2. Fill water tank with solution
3. Run brew cycle halfway, pause 15-20 minutes
4. Complete cycle, run solution through steam wand
5. Rinse thoroughly with 2-3 tanks of fresh water
6. Run a test shot before making coffee
Frequency: Quarterly (every 3 months). Use soft water to reduce scale buildup.

8. Why does my espresso taste bitter?

Causes: Over-extraction, water too hot, dark roast beans, or dirty equipment.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Coarsen grind or reduce extraction time
‚Ä¢ Verify water temperature (195-205¬∞F ideal)
‚Ä¢ Try medium roast beans
‚Ä¢ Deep clean all coffee contact surfaces

9. Why does my espresso taste sour?

Causes: Under-extraction, water too cool, light roast beans, or channeling.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Grind finer to increase extraction
‚Ä¢ Ensure machine fully heated (20+ minutes)
‚Ä¢ Improve distribution and tamping technique
‚Ä¢ Consider darker roast for less acidity

10. My grinder runs but no coffee comes out.

Causes: Burr jam from foreign object, broken burr carrier, worn burrs, or clogged chute.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Remove hopper and check for stones or debris
‚Ä¢ Clean thoroughly with brush
‚Ä¢ Inspect burrs for damage or wear
‚Ä¢ Replace burrs if edges are rounded (every 300-500 lbs)

11. Why is there no crema on my espresso?

Causes: Stale beans, incorrect grind, low pressure, or using pre-ground coffee.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Use beans roasted within 2-4 weeks
‚Ä¢ Ensure 9 bar brewing pressure
‚Ä¢ Grind just before brewing
‚Ä¢ Check machine pressure gauge during extraction

12. Why is my espresso machine making loud noises?

Causes: Vibration pump normal operation, worn pump, scale buildup, or loose components.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Some noise is normal for vibration pumps
‚Ä¢ Descale if grinding/whining sounds occur
‚Ä¢ Tighten loose panels or drip tray
‚Ä¢ Grinding noise may indicate pump replacement needed

13. My pressure gauge shows incorrect readings.

Causes: Faulty gauge, pressure relief valve issues, or actual pressure problems.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Test with blind basket to verify actual pressure
‚Ä¢ Clean or replace OPV (over-pressure valve)
‚Ä¢ Gauge replacement is usually simple DIY fix
‚Ä¢ Professional calibration may be needed

14. Machine keeps running and overfills shots.

Causes: Faulty flow meter, stuck button, or programming issue on automatic machines.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Reset to factory settings if programmable
‚Ä¢ Clean button contacts with alcohol
‚Ä¢ Manually stop shots until repaired
‚Ä¢ Flow meter may need replacement

15. Display shows error codes.

Common codes: Fill tank, descale needed, grinder blocked, or service required.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Consult manual for specific code meaning
‚Ä¢ Try power cycling (off for 30 seconds)
‚Ä¢ Perform indicated maintenance
‚Ä¢ Some codes require technician reset

16. Machine gets too hot or overheats.

Causes: Faulty thermostat, scale preventing heat dissipation, or PID controller malfunction.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Descale thoroughly
‚Ä¢ Check for blocked ventilation
‚Ä¢ Thermostat replacement often required
‚Ä¢ Don't use until repaired (fire hazard)

17. Portafilter is stuck or won't lock in.

Causes: Coffee grounds in threads, worn gasket, or bent portafilter ears.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Clean group head threads thoroughly
‚Ä¢ Replace gasket if over 1 year old
‚Ä¢ Check portafilter ears for damage
‚Ä¢ Never force - can damage machine

18. Machine won't turn on at all.

Causes: Power supply issue, blown fuse, faulty switch, or safety thermostat tripped.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Test outlet with another appliance
‚Ä¢ Check power cord for damage
‚Ä¢ Inspect internal fuse (unplug first)
‚Ä¢ Reset thermal safety switch if accessible

19. Coffee has metallic or strange taste.

Causes: New machine needs seasoning, scale buildup, or water quality issues.
Solution:
‚Ä¢ Run 10-20 shots through new machine
‚Ä¢ Descale quarterly and backflush regularly
‚Ä¢ Use soft water (not distilled or hard water)
‚Ä¢ Replace water filter if equipped

20. Should I repair or replace my machine?

Consider repair if:
‚Ä¢ Machine is under 5 years old
‚Ä¢ Repair cost is under 50% of replacement
‚Ä¢ It's a quality brand (Lelit, Breville, Rancilio, La Marzocco, Jura)
‚Ä¢ Only needs gaskets, seals, or descaling

Consider replacement if:
‚Ä¢ Over 10 years old with major issues
‚Ä¢ Multiple components failing
‚Ä¢ Repair exceeds 60% of new machine cost
‚Ä¢ Parts are discontinued

Common Grinder Problems & Solutions

1. Grinder runs but no coffee comes out:
‚Ä¢ Check for foreign objects (stones, sticks) jamming burrs
‚Ä¢ Inspect burr carrier for cracks
‚Ä¢ Clean clogged chute with brush
‚Ä¢ Replace worn burrs (edges should be sharp, not rounded)

2. Inconsistent grind size:
‚Ä¢ Worn burrs need replacement (every 300-500 lbs coffee)
‚Ä¢ Check burr alignment and calibration
‚Ä¢ Clean coffee oil buildup
‚Ä¢ Ensure hopper is properly seated

3. Grinder making loud grinding noise:
‚Ä¢ Foreign object in burrs (immediately stop and check)
‚Ä¢ Burrs touching (needs recalibration)
‚Ä¢ Worn burr carrier or motor bearings
‚Ä¢ Coffee beans too oily or stale

4. Grinder won't turn on:
‚Ä¢ Check safety switches (hopper, grounds bin)
‚Ä¢ Test thermal overload protection (let cool 30 min)
‚Ä¢ Inspect power cord and switch
‚Ä¢ Motor capacitor may need replacement

5. Coffee clumping or static:
‚Ä¢ Add 1-2 drops of water to beans (RDT method)
‚Ä¢ Use anti-static spray on catch bin
‚Ä¢ Check humidity levels (40-60% ideal)
‚Ä¢ Clean chute and exit point regularly

Grinder Maintenance Schedule

Daily:
‚Ä¢ Empty and wipe hopper
‚Ä¢ Brush out grounds from chute
‚Ä¢ Clean dosing area

Weekly:
‚Ä¢ Deep clean with grinder cleaning tablets
‚Ä¢ Remove and clean hopper thoroughly
‚Ä¢ Vacuum around burr chamber

Monthly:
‚Ä¢ Remove upper burr and clean both burrs
‚Ä¢ Check burr sharpness and alignment
‚Ä¢ Clean all coffee oil residue

Annually:
‚Ä¢ Professional service and calibration
‚Ä¢ Replace burrs if needed
‚Ä¢ Motor bearing inspection
